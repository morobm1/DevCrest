Housing Contract

Key terms and conditions for residency at Ivory University House

This page summarizes the primary terms of the Ivory University House Housing Contract. It is provided for information only and does not replace the official lease. The official lease you sign governs.

1. Overview and Purpose

Ivory University House provides student-focused housing to foster academic success, wellness, and community engagement in a substance‑free environment.

2. Eligibility

  • Active enrollment at a qualifying institution during the lease term.
  • Completion of the brief applicant interview process.
  • Agreement to the Core Value Agreement.

3. Term and Occupancy

  • Fixed lease term with defined start and end dates.
  • Single-resident occupancy per bed space; no overnight guests.
  • Check-in/out procedures and condition report required.

4. Payments and Fees

  • Monthly installments due per the payment schedule in your lease.
  • Security deposit due upon lease execution to reserve your space.
  • Late fees and other charges as specified in the official lease.

5. Resident Responsibilities

  • Maintain cleanliness and reasonable care of the unit and common areas.
  • Promptly report maintenance issues and safety concerns.
  • Comply with all community policies, notices, and staff instructions.

6. Community Standards and Prohibited Conduct

Residents must adhere to our substance‑free standards and safety practices outlined in the Core Value Agreement.

  • No possession, consumption, or distribution of alcohol, marijuana/cannabis, tobacco, smoking, or vaping products.
  • No gambling, pornographic material, or illegal activity.
  • No overnight guests.

7. Maintenance and Repairs

  • Submit maintenance requests through designated channels.
  • Non-emergency repairs performed during normal business hours.
  • Emergency maintenance available for urgent safety issues.

8. Termination and Cancellation

  • Early termination, reassignment, or subletting subject to lease.
  • Violation of the Core Values or lease terms may result in removal.
  • Move-out procedures and deposit reconciliation follow the lease.

9. Notices and Communication

  • Official notices may be delivered electronically or in writing.
  • Residents are responsible for keeping contact information current.

Disclaimer: This summary is informational. The signed lease is the controlling document. For a copy of your official lease, contact our office.
